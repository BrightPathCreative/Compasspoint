The first thing that changes isn’t the tech - it’s leadership.</p><p>In industries that haven’t gone through full digital transformation — trades, manufacturing, professional services, family-run businesses — automation can feel overwhelming. It challenges the way decisions have always been made. It exposes inefficiencies that were once invisible. And it forces clarity.</p><p>Who makes decisions now? Where does human judgment still matter most?</p><p>What does leadership actually focus on when routine tasks are automated?</p><p>I’m seeing a real divide.</p><p>The SMEs that thrive are redesigning around value — not hierarchy. They’re simplifying decision-making, strengthening their operating model, and investing in capability, not just cost savings.</p><p>But the deeper shift is cultural.</p><p>Automation changes how people feel about their roles. It can create fear — or it can create momentum.

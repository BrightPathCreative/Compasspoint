<p><strong>The Real Risk of AI Isn’t Job Loss — It’s Culture Loss</strong></p><p>I was reading Gallup’s 2025 <em>State of the Global Workplace</em> report recently, and one stat really stood out to me: Only <strong><em>21% of employees are engaged</em></strong> at work.</p><p>That means almost 8 in 10 people are just turning up, doing their job — but not really <em>feeling it.</em> Not feeling connected, motivated, or part of something meaningful. I would say that they are probably <strong><em>unhappy. </em></strong>Imagine how much productivity is lost as a result of unhappy and unengaged employees.</p><p>Now add AI into that mix. </p><p>Teams are no longer just human — we’re introducing intelligent agents that don’t have emotions, values, or a sense of belonging.</p><p>And yet, the <strong>one thing AI can’t replicate is culture.</strong></p><p>You can have the best systems, the fastest automation, and the smartest algorithms…<br />but if your people aren’t engaged — if your culture doesn’t inspire — the impact is always limited.</p><p>In my consulting work, I’ve seen this pattern again and again: <strong><em>AI transformation fails where culture isn’t ready for it.</em></strong></p><p>Because AI doesn’t replace leadership — it amplifies it.<br />It shines a light on the gaps between what we say we value… and what we actually reward, measure, and prioritise.</p><p>The challenge for every leader today isn’t just digital transformation — it’s <strong>cultural transformation.</strong></p><p>We need to ask:</p><p>·   	How do we keep people feeling connected, valued, and creative in a world powered by humans <em>and</em> algorithms?</p><p>·   	How do we make technology elevate our culture, not erode it?</p><p>·   	How do we build purpose when part of our workforce doesn’t have emotions or empathy?</p><p>These are the questions shaping the future of work.<br />And they’re what will separate good companies from great ones in the decade ahead.</p><p>Because technology may drive efficiency — but <strong>culture drives performance.<br /></strong>And the businesses that master both will define the next generation of success stories.</p><p>Remember, culture isn’t a “soft skill.”  It’s your most scalable system.</p><p>TOPIC: AI, Culture Transformation, Leadership, Future of Work, Digital Transformation</p><p>Date: 27 October 2025</p>
